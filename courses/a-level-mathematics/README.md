## üéì What You'll Learn

### Pure Mathematics

- **Algebra**: Polynomial division, factor theorem, partial fractions, proof
- **Functions**: Modulus, composite, inverse functions, transformations
- **Coordinate Geometry**: Circles, parametric equations, vectors
- **Sequences**: Arithmetic, geometric, binomial expansion
- **Trigonometry**: Identities, equations, small angle approximations, addition formulae
- **Exponentials & Logarithms**: Laws, equations, natural logarithms
- **Differentiation**: Product, quotient, chain rules, implicit, parametric, rates of change
- **Integration**: By substitution, by parts, partial fractions, volumes of revolution, differential equations

### Statistics

- **Data**: Sampling methods, types of data, measures of location and spread
- **Probability**: Venn diagrams, tree diagrams, conditional probability
- **Distributions**: Binomial, normal distributions, hypothesis testing
- **Correlation and Regression**: Scatter diagrams, product moment correlation, linear regression

### Mechanics

- **Kinematics**: Displacement, velocity, acceleration, motion graphs, SUVAT equations
- **Forces**: Newton's laws, resolving forces, friction, equilibrium
- **Moments**: Principle of moments, equilibrium problems
- **Projectiles**: 2D motion under constant acceleration

## üí° Key Topics

- **Proof**: Direct, by exhaustion, by contradiction, disproof by counter-example
- **Complex Numbers**: Operations, Argand diagrams, polar form (A2 only)
- **Calculus**: Techniques of differentiation and integration
- **Numerical Methods**: Root-finding, iterative methods
- **Vectors**: 2D and 3D, scalar product, vector equations of lines
- **Differential Equations**: First-order, separable variables
- **Hypothesis Testing**: Binomial, normal distribution tests

## üìù Exam Tips

**Calculator**:
- Know your calculator functions
- Use for checking, not replacing understanding
- Show working even when using calculator

**Proof Questions**:
- State what you're proving
- Show all logical steps
- Conclude clearly

**Integration**:
- Always add constant \( +c \) for indefinite integrals
- Check by differentiating your answer

**Sketching Graphs**:
- Label axes and intercepts
- Show asymptotes
- Indicate key points

**Accuracy**:
- Use at least 4 decimal places in working
- Round final answer as instructed (usually 3 sf or dp)
