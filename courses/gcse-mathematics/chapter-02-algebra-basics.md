# Chapter 2: Algebra Basics

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:
- Use algebraic notation and write expressions
- Expand brackets (including double brackets)
- Factorise expressions
- Substitute values into formulae
- Simplify algebraic expressions
- Apply index laws to algebraic terms

---

## 2.1 Algebraic Notation

### Basic Notation Rules

In algebra, we use letters to represent unknown values or variables:

- **Multiplication:** Don't write the × symbol
  - 5 × a = **5a**
  - a × b = **ab**
  - 2 × a × b = **2ab**

- **Division:** Write as fractions
  - a ÷ 5 = **a/5**
  - (a + b) ÷ 2 = **(a + b)/2**

- **Powers:** Use superscript
  - a × a = **a²**
  - a × a × a = **a³**

### Writing Expressions

**Example 1:** Write an expression for:
- Five more than n: **n + 5**
- Three less than x: **x − 3**
- Double y: **2y**
- Half of p: **p/2**
- The product of m and n: **mn**

**Example 2:** Jasmine thinks of a number n. She multiplies it by 4 and adds 7. Write an expression for her answer.

Answer: **4n + 7**

## 2.2 Collecting Like Terms

### Simplifying Expressions

Only **like terms** (same letter, same power) can be combined:

**Example 1:** Simplify 5a + 3b + 2a − b

Solution:
- Collect a terms: 5a + 2a = 7a
- Collect b terms: 3b − b = 2b
- **Answer: 7a + 2b**

**Example 2:** Simplify 4x² + 3x − 2x² + 5x

Solution:
- Collect x² terms: 4x² − 2x² = 2x²
- Collect x terms: 3x + 5x = 8x
- **Answer: 2x² + 8x**

### Important Points

- **4x and 4x²** are NOT like terms (different powers)
- **3ab and 3ba** are the same (order doesn't matter in multiplication)
- **5x and 5y** are NOT like terms (different letters)

---

## 2.3 Multiplying Terms

### Basic Multiplication

When multiplying terms, multiply the numbers and add the powers of like letters:

**Examples:**
- 3a × 4b = **12ab**
- 5x × 2x = **10x²**
- 3y² × 4y³ = **12y⁵**
- 2ab × 3a²b = **6a³b²**

### Dividing Terms

When dividing, divide the numbers and subtract the powers:

**Examples:**
- 12a ÷ 3 = **4a**
- 15x⁵ ÷ 3x² = **5x³**
- 20a²b³ ÷ 4ab = **5ab²**

## 2.4 Expanding Brackets

### Single Brackets

Multiply everything inside the bracket by the term outside:

**Example 1:** Expand 5(x + 3)

Solution: 5 × x + 5 × 3 = **5x + 15**

**Example 2:** Expand 3(2x − 4)

Solution: 3 × 2x + 3 × (−4) = **6x − 12**

**Example 3:** Expand −2(3x + 5)

Solution: −2 × 3x + (−2) × 5 = **−6x − 10**

**Example 4:** Expand and simplify 4(x + 2) + 3(x − 1)

Solution:
- 4x + 8 + 3x − 3
- Collect like terms: 4x + 3x + 8 − 3
- **Answer: 7x + 5**

### Double Brackets (FOIL Method)

**F**irst, **O**uter, **I**nner, **L**ast

**Example 1:** Expand (x + 3)(x + 5)

Solution:
- **F**irst: x × x = x²
- **O**uter: x × 5 = 5x
- **I**nner: 3 × x = 3x
- **L**ast: 3 × 5 = 15
- Combine: x² + 5x + 3x + 15
- **Answer: x² + 8x + 15**

**Example 2:** Expand (x + 4)(x − 2)

Solution:
- x² + (−2x) + 4x + (−8)
- x² + 2x − 8
- **Answer: x² + 2x − 8**

**Example 3:** Expand (2x + 3)(x − 4)

Solution:
- 2x × x = 2x²
- 2x × (−4) = −8x
- 3 × x = 3x
- 3 × (−4) = −12
- **Answer: 2x² − 5x − 12**

### Squaring Brackets

**Example:** Expand (x + 5)²

Remember: (x + 5)² = (x + 5)(x + 5)

Solution:
- x² + 5x + 5x + 25
- **Answer: x² + 10x + 25**

## 2.5 Factorising

Factorising is the reverse of expanding brackets.

### Single Brackets

Find the **highest common factor** (HCF) and put it outside the brackets:

**Example 1:** Factorise 6x + 9

Solution:
- HCF of 6 and 9 is 3
- 6x + 9 = **3(2x + 3)**
- Check: 3 × 2x + 3 × 3 = 6x + 9 ✓

**Example 2:** Factorise 12x² − 8x

Solution:
- HCF of 12x² and 8x is 4x
- 12x² − 8x = **4x(3x − 2)**

**Example 3:** Factorise 15a²b + 10ab²

Solution:
- HCF is 5ab
- **5ab(3a + 2b)**

### Quadratic Factorising (Higher Tier)

For expressions in the form x² + bx + c, find two numbers that:
- **Multiply** to give c
- **Add** to give b

**Example 1:** Factorise x² + 7x + 12

Solution:
- Need two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7
- 3 and 4 work (3 × 4 = 12, 3 + 4 = 7)
- **Answer: (x + 3)(x + 4)**

**Example 2:** Factorise x² − 5x + 6

Solution:
- Need two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to −5
- −2 and −3 work (−2 × −3 = 6, −2 + (−3) = −5)
- **Answer: (x − 2)(x − 3)**

**Example 3:** Factorise x² + 2x − 15

Solution:
- Need two numbers that multiply to −15 and add to 2
- 5 and −3 work (5 × −3 = −15, 5 + (−3) = 2)
- **Answer: (x + 5)(x − 3)**

## 2.6 Substitution

Substituting means replacing letters with numbers.

**Example 1:** If a = 5 and b = 3, find the value of:
(a) 2a + b
(b) a² − b²

Solution:
(a) 2(5) + 3 = 10 + 3 = **13**
(b) 5² − 3² = 25 − 9 = **16**

**Example 2:** The formula for the area of a trapezium is:
A = ½(a + b)h

Find A when a = 6, b = 10, and h = 5

Solution:
- A = ½(6 + 10) × 5
- A = ½ × 16 × 5
- A = 8 × 5
- **A = 40**

### Common Formula Substitutions

1. **Speed = Distance ÷ Time**
   - s = d/t

2. **Area of circle = πr²**
   - A = πr²

3. **Volume of cylinder = πr²h**
   - V = πr²h

## 2.7 Index Laws

### The Laws

When working with powers (indices):

1. **Multiplying:** Add the powers
   - aᵐ × aⁿ = **aᵐ⁺ⁿ**
   - Example: x³ × x⁵ = x⁸

2. **Dividing:** Subtract the powers
   - aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = **aᵐ⁻ⁿ**
   - Example: y⁷ ÷ y² = y⁵

3. **Power of a power:** Multiply the powers
   - (aᵐ)ⁿ = **aᵐⁿ**
   - Example: (x²)⁴ = x⁸

4. **Power of zero:** Any number to power 0 equals 1
   - a⁰ = **1**
   - Example: 5⁰ = 1, x⁰ = 1

5. **Negative powers:** Means reciprocal
   - a⁻ⁿ = **1/aⁿ**
   - Example: x⁻³ = 1/x³

### Worked Examples

**Example 1:** Simplify a⁴ × a³ ÷ a²

Solution:
- a⁴ × a³ = a⁷ (add powers)
- a⁷ ÷ a² = a⁵ (subtract powers)
- **Answer: a⁵**

**Example 2:** Simplify (3x²)³

Solution:
- 3³ × (x²)³
- 27 × x⁶
- **Answer: 27x⁶**

**Example 3:** Write 1/x⁴ using a negative power

Answer: **x⁻⁴**

**Example 4:** Simplify (2a³b²)²

Solution:
- 2² × (a³)² × (b²)²
- 4 × a⁶ × b⁴
- **Answer: 4a⁶b⁴**

## Key Points to Remember

✓ Only like terms can be added or subtracted
✓ When expanding, multiply everything inside the bracket
✓ When factorising, find the highest common factor
✓ FOIL helps with double brackets: First, Outer, Inner, Last
✓ When multiplying powers, add the indices
✓ When dividing powers, subtract the indices
✓ a⁰ = 1 and a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ
