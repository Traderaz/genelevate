## 10.1 Energy Efficiency in the Home

**Energy transfers in homes:**
- Heating: chemical/electrical ‚Üí thermal
- Lighting: electrical ‚Üí light (+ thermal waste)
- Appliances: electrical ‚Üí kinetic/thermal/light/sound

**Reducing energy waste:**

**Insulation:**
- Loft insulation (reduces conduction through roof)
- Cavity wall insulation (reduces convection)
- Double glazing (reduces conduction through windows)
- Draught excluders (reduces convection)

**Cost effectiveness:**
Payback time = Initial cost / Annual saving

**Example:** Loft insulation costs ¬£400, saves ¬£100/year
- Payback time = 400/100 = **4 years**

## 10.3 Power Stations

**Process:**
1. Fuel burned (or nuclear fission) heats water
2. Water boils ‚Üí steam
3. Steam turns turbine
4. Turbine turns generator
5. Generator produces electricity

**Efficiency:**
- Thermal power stations: ~35-45%
- Much energy lost as waste heat
- Combined heat and power (CHP) more efficient

## 10.4 The National Grid

**Components:**
- Power stations
- Step-up transformers (to 400,000V)
- Transmission lines (pylons)
- Step-down transformers (to 230V)
- Homes and businesses

**Why high voltage?**
- High V ‚Üí low I (for same power)
- Power loss = I¬≤R
- Lower current ‚Üí less energy wasted as heat
- More efficient transmission

**Example:** 10MW transmitted at 250,000V vs 25,000V
- At 250,000V: I = 40A
- At 25,000V: I = 400A

## 10.5 Static Electricity

**Caused by friction:**
- Electrons transferred between objects
- One becomes positive, one negative
- Like charges repel, opposite attract

**Examples:**
- Rubbing balloon on hair
- Lightning (charge builds in clouds)
- Spark when touching metal after walking on carpet

**Uses:**
- **Photocopiers:** Attract toner to paper
- **Spray painting:** Charged paint attracted to object (even coating)
- **Dust precipitators:** Remove smoke particles

**Dangers:**
- Aircraft refueling: static spark could ignite fuel
- Grain silos: grain dust explosion
- Electronic components: can be damaged

## 10.6 Electric Fields

**Around charged objects**

**Field lines:**
- Show direction of force on positive charge
- Point away from positive, towards negative
- Closer lines = stronger field

**Force on charged particle:**
- Opposite charges attract
- Like charges repel
- Force stronger when closer

## 10.9 Domestic Electricity Costs

**Energy (kWh) = Power (kW) √ó Time (hours)**

**Cost = Energy (kWh) √ó Price per kWh**

**Example:** 2kW heater for 3 hours, electricity costs 15p/kWh
- Energy = 2 √ó 3 = 6 kWh
- Cost = 6 √ó 15p = **90p**

## 10.10 Electrical Safety

**Fuses and circuit breakers:**
- Break circuit if current too high
- Prevent fires from overheating wires

**Earthing:**
- Metal case connected to earth wire
- If live wire touches case, large current flows
- Fuse blows, breaking circuit
- Prevents electric shock

**Double insulation:**
- Plastic casing (insulator)
- No metal parts exposed
- No earth wire needed

**RCD (Residual Current Device):**
- Detects difference in current between live and neutral
- Switches off very quickly
- Prevents electrocution
