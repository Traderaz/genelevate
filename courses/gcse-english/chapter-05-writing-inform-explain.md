# Chapter 5: Writing to Inform and Explain

## Purpose
**Inform:** Give information clearly
**Explain:** Make something understood

## Structure
**Title:** Clear and informative
**Introduction:** Overview of topic
**Paragraphs:** Each covers one point
**Conclusion:** Summary

## Language Features
- Clear, concise language
- Formal tone
- Present tense
- Connectives (firstly, additionally, finally)
- Technical vocabulary when appropriate
- Definitions for complex terms

## Organizational Techniques
- **Subheadings:** Break up text
- **Bullet points:** List information
- **Numbers:** Step-by-step instructions
- **Topic sentences:** Start each paragraph
- **Examples:** Clarify points

## Difference: Inform vs Explain
**Inform:** "The Amazon rainforest covers 5.5 million km²"
**Explain:** "The Amazon is called the 'lungs of the Earth' because its trees produce 20% of our oxygen"
