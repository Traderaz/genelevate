# Chapter 4: Writing to Argue and Persuade

## Purpose
Convince readers of your viewpoint using evidence and persuasive techniques.

## Structure
1. **Introduction:** State your viewpoint clearly
2. **Paragraphs 2-4:** Present arguments with evidence
3. **Counter-argument:** Address opposing view, then refute it
4. **Conclusion:** Reinforce your viewpoint, call to action

## Persuasive Techniques
- **Rhetorical questions:** "Can we really ignore this?"
- **Emotive language:** "devastating consequences"
- **Facts and statistics:** "75% of people agree"
- **Expert opinions:** "Scientists confirm..."
- **Anecdotes:** Personal stories
- **Direct address:** "You must act"
- **Repetition:** Emphasis
- **Rule of three:** "Safe, effective, essential"

## Language Features
- Strong opinion words (essential, vital, crucial)
- Modal verbs (must, should, need to)
- Connectives (furthermore, moreover, however)
- Formal tone
- Present tense

## Example Structure
**Introduction:** "It is undeniable that climate change poses the greatest threat to humanity."
**Argument 1:** Evidence about rising temperatures
**Argument 2:** Impact on wildlife
**Argument 3:** Economic consequences
**Counter-argument:** "Some argue change is too costly, but..."
**Conclusion:** "We must act now to secure our future."
