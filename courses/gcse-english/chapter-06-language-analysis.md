# Chapter 6: Language Analysis

## Word Classes
- **Nouns:** Names (person, place, thing)
- **Verbs:** Actions/states
- **Adjectives:** Describe nouns
- **Adverbs:** Describe verbs
- **Pronouns:** Replace nouns (he, she, they)
- **Prepositions:** Position/time (in, on, under)
- **Conjunctions:** Join words/clauses (and, but, because)

## Sentence Types
**Simple:** One clause ("The dog barked")
**Compound:** Two independent clauses ("The dog barked and the cat ran")
**Complex:** Main clause + subordinate ("When the dog barked, the cat ran")

## Language Devices
- **Metaphor:** Direct comparison
- **Simile:** Comparison using like/as
- **Personification:** Human qualities to non-human
- **Alliteration:** Repeated sounds
- **Onomatopoeia:** Sound words
- **Hyperbole:** Exaggeration
- **Imagery:** Vivid descriptions

## Analyzing Effects
**Formula:** Device + Quote + Effect
"The writer uses a metaphor, 'time is money,' suggesting time is valuable."
