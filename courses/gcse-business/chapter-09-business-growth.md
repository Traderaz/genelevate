# Chapter 9: Business Growth

## Learning Objectives
- Understand reasons for business growth
- Explain methods of growth (internal and external)
- Analyze benefits and drawbacks of growth
- Understand economies and diseconomies of scale
- Evaluate different growth strategies
- Examine the role of globalization

## 9.1 Why Businesses Grow

### Reasons for Growth

**1. Increased Profits**
- Larger sales revenue
- Economies of scale reduce costs
- Higher overall profit

**2. Market Power**
- Increased market share
- Greater influence over suppliers and customers
- Competitive advantage

**3. Economies of Scale**
- Lower average costs
- Improved efficiency
- Better profit margins

**4. Diversification**
- Spread risk across products/markets
- Reduce dependence on single product
- Protect against market decline

**5. Survival**
- Compete with larger rivals
- Avoid takeover
- Respond to market changes

**6. Owner Ambition**
- Personal satisfaction
- Status and prestige
- Leave legacy

### Measuring Business Size

**1. Revenue (Sales Turnover)**
- Total income from sales
- Doesn't account for profitability

**2. Number of Employees**
- Indicates scale of operations
- Some industries more labor-intensive

**3. Market Share**
- Percentage of total market
- Shows competitive position

**4. Market Capitalization (PLCs)**
- Share price × number of shares
- Value of company on stock market

**5. Capital Employed**
- Total assets - current liabilities
- Shows investment in business

**EU Business Size Classifications**:
- **Micro**: 0-9 employees, <€2m turnover
- **Small**: 10-49 employees, <€10m turnover
- **Medium**: 50-249 employees, <€50m turnover
- **Large**: 250+ employees, €50m+ turnover

## 9.2 Internal (Organic) Growth

### Definition

**Internal/Organic growth** = Expansion from within the business using own resources

### Methods

**1. Increase Output**
- Expand production capacity
- Increase efficiency
- Open more locations

**2. New Products**
- Develop and launch new products
- Extend product range
- Innovation

**3. New Markets**
- Export to new countries
- Target new customer segments
- Online sales channels

**4. Increase Market Share**
- Aggressive marketing
- Competitive pricing
- Improve quality

### Advantages of Internal Growth

✓ **Less risky**: Gradual, controlled expansion  
✓ **Retain control**: Owners maintain full control  
✓ **Use existing strengths**: Build on current expertise  
✓ **No integration issues**: Avoid cultural clashes  
✓ **Maintain brand identity**: Consistent values  
✓ **Cheaper**: No acquisition costs

### Disadvantages of Internal Growth

❌ **Slow**: Takes years to achieve significant growth  
❌ **Limited by resources**: Constrained by finance and capacity  
❌ **Dependent on market growth**: Can't grow faster than market  
❌ **Missed opportunities**: Competitors may grow faster  
❌ **Risk of overtrading**: Growing too fast strains resources

## 9.3 External Growth

### Definition

**External growth** = Expansion through mergers and acquisitions (joining with or buying other businesses)

### Merger vs Acquisition

**Merger**: Two businesses voluntarily join to form new entity  
**Acquisition (Takeover)**: One business buys another

### Types of Integration

#### 1. Horizontal Integration

**Definition**: Joining with business at same stage of production in same industry

**Example**: Two supermarkets merging (Sainsbury's attempted merger with Asda)

**Advantages**:
✓ Increased market share  
✓ Reduced competition  
✓ Economies of scale  
✓ Combined expertise  
✓ Larger customer base

**Disadvantages**:
❌ May face regulatory issues (monopoly concerns)  
❌ Integration challenges  
❌ Potential job losses (duplication)  
❌ Cultural clashes

#### 2. Vertical Integration

**Definition**: Joining with business at different stage of production in same industry

**Forward Integration**: Acquire business closer to customer

**Example**: Manufacturer buys retailer (Apple stores selling Apple products)

**Advantages**:
✓ Control over distribution  
✓ Direct customer access  
✓ Retail profit margins  
✓ Better customer feedback

**Backward Integration**: Acquire supplier

**Example**: Car manufacturer buys parts supplier

**Advantages**:
✓ Secure supply  
✓ Control quality  
✓ Lower costs  
✓ Reliable delivery

**General Advantages of Vertical Integration**:
✓ Control entire supply chain  
✓ Capture more profit margins  
✓ Reduce costs  
✓ Ensure quality standards

**General Disadvantages**:
❌ Different expertise required  
❌ Higher risk (less diversification)  
❌ May upset other suppliers/retailers  
❌ Complex management

#### 3. Conglomerate Integration (Diversification)

**Definition**: Joining with business in completely different industry

**Example**: Virgin Group (airlines, trains, telecoms, hotels, gyms, banks)

**Advantages**:
✓ Spread risk across industries  
✓ Opportunities in growing sectors  
✓ Cross-selling opportunities  
✓ Financial stability (if one sector declines)

**Disadvantages**:
❌ Lack of expertise in new industry  
❌ Complex to manage  
❌ Difficult to achieve synergies  
❌ Diluted focus

### Advantages of External Growth

✓ **Fast growth**: Immediate increase in size  
✓ **Market share**: Instant market presence  
✓ **Acquire assets**: Gain resources, customers, expertise  
✓ **Reduce competition**: Eliminate rivals  
✓ **Synergies**: Combined efficiency ("2+2=5")

### Disadvantages of External Growth

❌ **Very expensive**: Acquisitions cost millions  
❌ **Integration problems**: Different cultures, systems  
❌ **Hidden problems**: Acquire debts or issues  
❌ **Job losses**: Redundancies damage morale  
❌ **Regulatory issues**: May be blocked by competition authorities  
❌ **Dis-synergies**: May not achieve expected benefits ("2+2=3")

## 9.4 Franchising as Growth Strategy

### Definition

**Franchising** = Business (franchisor) allows others (franchisees) to use brand and business model

### For Franchisor (Expanding Business)

**Advantages**:
✓ Rapid expansion with low investment  
✓ Franchise fees and royalties  
✓ Motivated franchisees (own investment)  
✓ Local market knowledge

**Disadvantages**:
❌ Less control over quality  
❌ Share profits (royalties)  
❌ Reputation risk (poor franchisee)  
❌ Support costs

**Examples**: McDonald's, Subway, Costa Coffee

## 9.5 Economies of Scale

### Definition

**Economies of scale** = Cost advantages from increased scale of operation (average cost per unit decreases as output increases)

### Types of Economies of Scale

#### 1. Purchasing Economies

**Definition**: Bulk buying discounts from suppliers

**Example**: Tesco orders millions of products, negotiates lower prices per unit than small shop

**Benefit**: Lower cost per unit

#### 2. Technical Economies

**Definition**: Large-scale production technology more efficient

**Example**: Automated production line only cost-effective for high volumes

**Benefit**: Lower production costs, higher quality

#### 3. Managerial Economies

**Definition**: Specialist managers increase efficiency

**Example**: Large business can afford specialist finance director; small business owner does all management

**Benefit**: Expertise improves decision-making and efficiency

#### 4. Financial Economies

**Definition**: Larger businesses get better loan terms

**Example**: PLCs get lower interest rates than small businesses

**Benefit**: Lower finance costs

#### 5. Marketing Economies

**Definition**: Marketing costs spread over larger output

**Example**: TV ad costs £100,000 whether selling 1,000 or 1 million units

**Benefit**: Lower marketing cost per unit

#### 6. Risk-Bearing Economies

**Definition**: Diversification spreads risk

**Example**: Large business with multiple products less vulnerable than single-product business

**Benefit**: Reduced business risk

### Benefits of Economies of Scale

✓ Lower average costs  
✓ Higher profit margins  
✓ Can lower prices (competitive advantage)  
✓ Invest more in R&D  
✓ Better negotiating power

## 9.6 Diseconomies of Scale

### Definition

**Diseconomies of scale** = Cost disadvantages from being too large (average cost per unit increases as business gets too big)

### Causes of Diseconomies of Scale

#### 1. Communication Problems

**Issue**: Information doesn't flow efficiently in large organizations

**Result**:
- Delays in decision-making
- Misunderstandings
- Duplication of work
- Mistakes

#### 2. Motivation Issues

**Issue**: Employees feel less valued in large organization

**Result**:
- Lower productivity
- Higher absenteeism
- More staff turnover
- Poor quality work

#### 3. Coordination Difficulties

**Issue**: Hard to coordinate activities across large organization

**Result**:
- Inefficiency
- Departments working against each other
- Wasted resources
- Slower response to problems

#### 4. Control Problems

**Issue**: Management can't oversee everything

**Result**:
- Quality issues
- Fraud or theft
- Inefficient processes
- Lack of accountability

### Solutions to Diseconomies of Scale

**1. Delayering**
- Remove management layers
- Speed up communication
- Empower employees

**2. Restructuring**
- Create smaller business units
- Give autonomy to divisions
- Improve accountability

**3. Technology**
- Better communication systems
- Project management software
- Data analytics for monitoring

**4. Motivation Programs**
- Recognition schemes
- Profit-sharing
- Employee engagement

## 9.7 Problems of Growth

### Overtrading

**Definition**: Growing too quickly without sufficient capital

**Symptoms**:
- Cash flow problems
- Unable to pay suppliers
- Declining quality
- Overstretched resources

**Solutions**:
- Slow growth
- Secure additional finance
- Improve cash flow management

### Loss of Control

**Issue**: Owners lose direct oversight as business grows

**Problems**:
- Decision-making slows
- Quality standards slip
- Culture dilutes
- Strategic direction unclear

**Solutions**:
- Strong management systems
- Clear delegation
- Regular monitoring
- Maintain company culture

### Cultural Clashes (Mergers/Acquisitions)

**Issue**: Different organizational cultures conflict

**Problems**:
- Employee resistance
- Low morale
- High turnover
- Failure to achieve synergies

**Solutions**:
- Careful integration planning
- Communication with all staff
- Respect both cultures
- Find common ground

## 9.8 Globalization

### What is Globalization?

**Globalization** = Increasing interconnection of world economies through trade, investment, and technology

### Drivers of Globalization

1. **Technology**: Internet, communication advances
2. **Transportation**: Cheaper, faster shipping and air freight
3. **Trade liberalization**: Reduced tariffs and barriers
4. **Multinational corporations**: Operate in many countries
5. **Emerging economies**: Growth of China, India, Brazil

### Opportunities from Globalization

**For Businesses**:
✓ Access to larger markets  
✓ Lower production costs (outsourcing)  
✓ Economies of scale  
✓ Spread risk geographically  
✓ Access to resources and talent worldwide

**For Consumers**:
✓ Greater product choice  
✓ Lower prices  
✓ Access to international brands  
✓ Improved quality (global competition)

### Threats from Globalization

**For Businesses**:
❌ Increased competition (from global rivals)  
❌ Complex operations (cultural, legal differences)  
❌ Currency fluctuations  
❌ Political instability in some regions  
❌ Reputation risks (supply chain ethics)

**For Consumers**:
❌ Job losses (outsourcing)  
❌ Exploitation in developing countries  
❌ Environmental damage  
❌ Loss of local businesses

**For Countries**:
❌ Dependence on global economy  
❌ Vulnerable to external shocks  
❌ Loss of cultural identity  
❌ Inequality between rich and poor nations

### Multinational Corporations (MNCs)

**Definition**: Companies operating in multiple countries

**Examples**: Apple, McDonald's, Toyota, Unilever

**Benefits to Host Countries**:
✓ Job creation  
✓ Investment in infrastructure  
✓ Tax revenue  
✓ Technology transfer  
✓ Skills development

**Costs to Host Countries**:
❌ Profits leave country  
❌ May exploit workers  
❌ Environmental damage  
❌ Dominate local businesses  
❌ Political influence

## 9.9 Growth Strategies

### Ansoff's Matrix

**Framework for growth strategies**:

```
                 EXISTING PRODUCTS | NEW PRODUCTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXISTING      │                  │
MARKETS       │ MARKET           │ PRODUCT
              │ PENETRATION      │ DEVELOPMENT
              │                  │
              │ (Low Risk)       │ (Medium Risk)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NEW           │                  │
MARKETS       │ MARKET           │ DIVERSIFICATION
              │ DEVELOPMENT      │
              │                  │
              │ (Medium Risk)    │ (High Risk)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

**1. Market Penetration** (Low Risk)
- Increase sales of existing products in existing markets
- Methods: Price reductions, increased promotion, loyalty schemes
- Example: Coca-Cola increasing marketing spend

**2. Product Development** (Medium Risk)
- Launch new products in existing markets
- Methods: R&D, innovation, product variations
- Example: Apple launching new iPhone models

**3. Market Development** (Medium Risk)
- Sell existing products in new markets
- Methods: Export, target new segments, new distribution channels
- Example: UK supermarket expanding to Europe

**4. Diversification** (High Risk)
- New products in new markets
- Highest risk but spreads risk across markets
- Example: Amazon expanding from books to everything

## Key Terminology

- **Internal (Organic) Growth**: Expansion from within using own resources
- **External Growth**: Expansion through mergers/acquisitions
- **Merger**: Two businesses join voluntarily
- **Acquisition**: One business buys another
- **Horizontal Integration**: Joining with competitor at same stage
- **Vertical Integration**: Joining with supplier or customer
- **Backward Integration**: Buying supplier
- **Forward Integration**: Buying customer/retailer
- **Conglomerate**: Joining with business in different industry
- **Economies of Scale**: Cost advantages from larger scale
- **Diseconomies of Scale**: Cost disadvantages from being too large
- **Overtrading**: Growing too quickly without sufficient capital
- **Globalization**: Increasing interconnection of world economies
- **MNC**: Multinational corporation operating in many countries
- **Diversification**: Expanding into different products/markets

### Case Study Question

**Case Study: BakeFresh Ltd**

BakeFresh is a successful regional bakery with 15 stores in Southeast England. The business is considering three growth options:

**Option A**: Open 20 new stores over 3 years (internal growth)  
**Option B**: Acquire competitor CrustyCo (10 stores) for £5 million  
**Option C**: Acquire a flour mill to secure supply (backward vertical integration)

Current situation:
- Revenue: £8 million annually
- Profit: £800,000
- 200 employees
- Strong brand reputation
- Reliable flour supplier but prices increasing 10% annually

**Questions**:

a) State which type of integration Option C represents. (1 mark)

b) Explain one advantage of Option A (internal growth). (3 marks)

c) Analyze two economies of scale BakeFresh might achieve through growth. (6 marks)

d) Evaluate which growth option (A, B, or C) would be most suitable for BakeFresh. Justify your recommendation. (12 marks)

✅ Economies of scale: purchasing, technical, managerial, financial, marketing  
✅ Larger scale → lower average costs → competitive advantage  
✅ Diseconomies: communication, motivation, coordination problems from being too large  
✅ Overtrading = growing too fast without capital → cash flow problems  
✅ Globalization: opportunities (markets, lower costs) and threats (competition, complexity)  
✅ Ansoff's Matrix: penetration (low risk) → diversification (high risk)
