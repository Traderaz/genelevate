## 10.1 Economic Influences

### The Economic Climate

**Business activity is significantly affected by the state of the economy**

### Economic Growth and Recession

#### Economic Growth (Boom)

**Characteristics**:
- Rising GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
- Increasing employment
- Rising consumer confidence
- Higher consumer spending

**Impact on Businesses**:
‚úì **Increased sales**: Consumers spend more  
‚úì **Higher profits**: Revenue increases  
‚úì **Business confidence**: Investment in expansion  
‚úì **New businesses**: Entrepreneurs see opportunities

‚ùå **Increased competition**: More businesses enter market  
‚ùå **Rising costs**: Wages and raw materials more expensive  
‚ùå **Recruitment challenges**: Labor shortages

**Business Strategies**:
- Expand operations
- Invest in new products
- Hire more staff
- Increase prices cautiously

#### Recession (Economic Downturn)

**Characteristics**:
- Falling GDP (two consecutive quarters of negative growth)
- Rising unemployment
- Low consumer confidence
- Reduced consumer spending

**Impact on Businesses**:
‚ùå **Falling sales**: Consumers cut spending  
‚ùå **Lower profits**: Revenue decreases  
‚ùå **Business closures**: Unable to survive  
‚ùå **Redundancies**: Cost-cutting measures

‚úì **Lower costs**: Wages and materials may be cheaper  
‚úì **Less competition**: Weaker businesses exit market

**Business Strategies**:
- Cut costs
- Focus on value products
- Improve efficiency
- Diversify to resilient markets
- Maintain cash reserves

### Interest Rates

**Definition**: Cost of borrowing money (or reward for saving)

#### Impact of High Interest Rates

**For Businesses**:
‚ùå Borrowing more expensive (loans, mortgages)  
‚ùå Expansion becomes costly  
‚ùå Customers have less to spend (mortgage costs increase)  
‚ùå Reduced consumer demand

‚úì Savers have more to spend (some benefit)

**Business Response**:
- Delay investment
- Focus on internal finance
- Reduce prices to maintain sales

#### Impact of Low Interest Rates

**For Businesses**:
‚úì Borrowing cheaper (easier to expand)  
‚úì Customers have more disposable income  
‚úì Increased consumer demand  
‚úì Encourages investment

‚ùå Savers have less to spend

**Business Response**:
- Invest in growth
- Borrow for expansion
- Premium products may sell better

### Exchange Rates

**Definition**: Value of one currency against another

**Example**: ¬£1 = $1.30 or ¬£1 = ‚Ç¨1.17

#### Strong Pound (Appreciation)

**¬£1 = $1.50 (was $1.30)**

**Effects**:
‚úì **Imports cheaper**: Buy foreign goods for less  
‚úì **Good for businesses importing raw materials**: Lower costs

‚ùå **Exports more expensive**: Foreign buyers pay more  
‚ùå **Bad for exporters**: May lose sales abroad

**Example**: UK manufacturer exports to USA
- Product costs ¬£100 to produce
- At ¬£1=$1.30: Sells for $130
- At ¬£1=$1.50: Sells for $150 (less competitive)

#### Weak Pound (Depreciation)

**¬£1 = $1.10 (was $1.30)**

**Effects**:
‚úì **Exports cheaper**: More competitive abroad  
‚úì **Good for exporters**: May increase sales

‚ùå **Imports more expensive**: Foreign goods cost more  
‚ùå **Bad for importers**: Higher costs

**Business Strategies**:
- **Strong pound**: Source from abroad, focus on domestic market
- **Weak pound**: Expand exports, find domestic suppliers

### Unemployment

#### High Unemployment

**Effects on Business**:
‚úì Larger pool of applicants  
‚úì Lower wage demands  
‚úì Less staff turnover

‚ùå Reduced consumer spending  
‚ùå Lower demand for products

#### Low Unemployment

**Effects on Business**:
‚úì More consumer spending  
‚úì Higher demand

‚ùå Difficult to recruit  
‚ùå Higher wages needed  
‚ùå Training costs increase

### Inflation

**Definition**: General increase in prices over time

#### Impact of High Inflation

**For Businesses**:
‚ùå Rising costs (materials, wages, rent)  
‚ùå Uncertainty in planning  
‚ùå Need frequent price changes  
‚ùå Wage demands increase  
‚ùå Reduced purchasing power (customers)

**Business Response**:
- Increase prices (carefully)
- Negotiate fixed-price contracts
- Improve efficiency to offset costs
- Reduce costs where possible

#### Impact of Low/Stable Inflation

‚úì Predictable costs  
‚úì Easier planning  
‚úì Stable prices  
‚úì Consumer confidence

## 10.3 Legal and Regulatory Environment

### Employment Law

#### Key Legislation

**1. Equality Act 2010**
- No discrimination (age, gender, race, disability, religion, sexual orientation)
- Equal pay for equal work
- Reasonable adjustments for disabilities

**Impact**:
‚úì Fair treatment of all employees  
‚ùå Compliance costs, potential legal action

**2. National Minimum Wage/Living Wage**
- Minimum hourly rates by age
- Reviewed annually

**Impact**:
‚úì Fair wages, reduced poverty  
‚ùå Increased labor costs for businesses

**3. Employment Rights Act 1996**
- Written employment contracts
- Notice periods
- Protection from unfair dismissal
- Redundancy rights

**Impact**:
‚úì Job security, fair treatment  
‚ùå Less flexibility for employers

**4. Health and Safety at Work Act 1974**
- Employers must provide safe workplace
- Risk assessments
- Training and protective equipment
- Report accidents

**Impact**:
‚úì Safer workplaces, fewer injuries  
‚ùå Compliance costs, administration

**5. Working Time Regulations 1998**
- Maximum 48-hour working week (can opt out)
- Rest breaks
- Minimum 28 days paid holiday (including bank holidays)

**Impact**:
‚úì Work-life balance  
‚ùå Scheduling challenges, costs

### Consumer Protection Law

#### Key Legislation

**1. Consumer Rights Act 2015**
- Products must be:
  - Of satisfactory quality
  - Fit for purpose
  - As described
- Right to refund, repair, or replacement
- Digital content protection

**Impact**:
‚úì Consumer confidence  
‚ùå Return costs, reputation management

**2. Sale of Goods Act 1979 (updated by Consumer Rights Act)**
- Similar protections for goods

**3. Data Protection Act 2018 / GDPR**
- Protect personal data
- Customer consent required
- Right to be forgotten
- Report data breaches

**Impact**:
‚úì Customer trust, data security  
‚ùå Compliance costs, admin burden

**4. Trade Descriptions Act 1968**
- Honest product descriptions
- No false advertising

**Impact**:
‚úì Fair trading  
‚ùå Careful marketing required

### Environmental Legislation

**Key Laws**:
- **Climate Change Act 2008**: Reduce greenhouse gas emissions
- **Environmental Protection Act 1990**: Control pollution
- **Waste Regulations**: Proper disposal and recycling
- **Emissions Standards**: Limits on pollution

**Impact**:
‚úì Environmental protection  
‚ùå Compliance costs, operational changes

### Competition Law

**Competition Act 1998 / Enterprise Act 2002**

**Prohibits**:
- Anti-competitive agreements (price fixing, market sharing)
- Abuse of dominant position
- Anti-competitive mergers

**Enforced by**: Competition and Markets Authority (CMA)

**Impact**:
‚úì Fair competition, consumer choice  
‚ùå Merger restrictions, investigations costly

## 10.5 Competitive Environment

### Porter's Five Forces

**Framework for analyzing competitive environment**:

#### 1. Threat of New Entrants

**High threat if**:
- Low barriers to entry (cheap setup)
- High profit potential
- Easy access to distribution

**Low threat if**:
- High capital requirements
- Strong brand loyalty
- Economies of scale needed
- Patents or licenses required

#### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers

**High power if**:
- Few suppliers available
- Unique or differentiated products
- High switching costs
- Supplier can forward integrate

**Low power if**:
- Many suppliers
- Standardized products
- Easy to switch
- Business is large customer

#### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers

**High power if**:
- Few, large customers
- Standardized products
- Low switching costs
- Customers well-informed

**Low power if**:
- Many small customers
- Unique products
- High switching costs
- Strong brand loyalty

#### 4. Threat of Substitutes

**High threat if**:
- Many alternatives available
- Substitutes are cheaper
- Easy to switch
- Similar performance

**Low threat if**:
- Few alternatives
- Unique product
- High switching costs
- Superior performance

#### 5. Competitive Rivalry

**Intense if**:
- Many competitors
- Slow market growth
- Similar products
- High exit barriers
- Low switching costs

**Less intense if**:
- Few competitors
- Fast market growth
- Differentiated products
- Easy exit

### Competitive Strategies

**1. Cost Leadership**
- Lowest prices in market
- High volume, low margins
- Economies of scale
- Example: Primark, Ryanair

**2. Differentiation**
- Unique products or brand
- Premium pricing
- Quality or innovation
- Example: Apple, Dyson

**3. Focus/Niche**
- Target specific segment
- Specialized products
- Build expertise
- Example: Rolex, Lush Cosmetics

## 10.7 PESTLE Analysis

**Framework for analyzing external environment**:

**P - Political**: Government policies, stability, taxation, trade  
**E - Economic**: Growth, interest rates, inflation, unemployment  
**S - Social**: Demographics, lifestyles, values, culture  
**T - Technological**: Innovation, automation, digital transformation  
**L - Legal**: Employment, consumer, competition, environmental law  
**E - Environmental**: Climate change, sustainability, pollution

## Key Terminology

- **Economic Growth**: Increase in GDP
- **Recession**: Two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth
- **Interest Rate**: Cost of borrowing money
- **Exchange Rate**: Value of currency against another
- **Inflation**: General increase in prices
- **Unemployment**: People without jobs seeking work
- **GDPR**: General Data Protection Regulation
- **CSR**: Corporate Social Responsibility
- **Fair Trade**: Ethical trading ensuring fair prices for producers
- **Porter's Five Forces**: Framework analyzing competitive environment
- **PESTLE**: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental analysis
- **ESG**: Environmental, Social, Governance criteria
- **Greenwashing**: False claims of environmental responsibility
