### Qualitative vs Quantitative Data

| Qualitative Data | Quantitative Data |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Opinions, feelings, motivations | Numbers and statistics |
| Why and how | How many and how much |
| In-depth insights | Measurable facts |
| Focus groups, interviews | Surveys, sales data |
| Small samples | Large samples |
| Difficult to analyze | Easy to analyze statistically |

### Sampling

**Sampling** = Selecting a representative group from target population

**Sample size considerations**:
- **Larger sample**: More reliable but more expensive
- **Smaller sample**: Cheaper but less reliable

**Sampling methods**:
- **Random**: Everyone has equal chance
- **Stratified**: Divided by characteristics (age, gender)
- **Quota**: Specific numbers from each group
- **Convenience**: Easiest to reach

### 2. Price

**Definition**: Amount customers pay for product

#### Pricing Strategies

**Cost-Plus Pricing**
- Calculate costs + add markup
- Formula: Cost + (Cost Ã— Markup %)
- Simple but ignores competition and demand

**Competitive Pricing**
- Match or undercut competitors
- Used in highly competitive markets
- Risk of price wars

**Penetration Pricing**
- Low initial price to gain market share
- Increase price later
- Good for new products entering competitive markets

**Skimming Pricing**
- High initial price for innovation
- Lower price over time
- Good for unique, innovative products

**Psychological Pricing**
- Â£9.99 instead of Â£10
- Creates perception of better value

**Dynamic Pricing**
- Prices change based on demand
- Common in airlines, hotels, Uber

#### Factors Affecting Price

- **Costs**: Must cover costs to be profitable
- **Competition**: What rivals charge
- **Demand**: How much customers want product
- **Product life cycle**: New vs established products
- **Brand image**: Premium brands charge more
- **Objectives**: Market share vs profit maximization

### 3. Place (Distribution)

**Definition**: How product reaches customer

#### Distribution Channels

**Channel 1**: Producer â†’ Consumer (Direct)
- Example: Online sales, farmers' markets
- Advantages: Full control, higher margins
- Disadvantages: Need own distribution network

**Channel 2**: Producer â†’ Retailer â†’ Consumer
- Example: Manufacturer sells to supermarket
- Advantages: Retailers' expertise and customer base
- Disadvantages: Share profits with retailer

**Channel 3**: Producer â†’ Wholesaler â†’ Retailer â†’ Consumer
- Example: Small manufacturers using wholesalers
- Advantages: Reach many retailers
- Disadvantages: Lowest margins

#### Distribution Methods

**Intensive Distribution**
- Available in many outlets
- Example: Coca-Cola, newspapers
- Good for: Convenience products

**Selective Distribution**
- Available in selected outlets
- Example: Nike shoes in specific stores
- Good for: Shopping products

**Exclusive Distribution**
- Limited to one or few outlets per area
- Example: Designer fashion, luxury cars
- Good for: Specialty products

#### Promotional Methods

#### 1. Development

- Research and design
- No sales revenue
- High costs
- Losses

**Strategy**: R&D, market research

#### 2. Introduction

- Product launched
- Low sales
- High promotion costs
- Losses or low profit

**Strategy**: Build awareness, penetration pricing, selective distribution

#### 3. Growth

- Rapid sales increase
- Competition emerges
- Profits increase
- Economies of scale

**Strategy**: Expand distribution, build brand loyalty, improve quality

#### 4. Maturity

- Sales peak
- Market saturated
- Intense competition
- Profits peak then decline

**Strategy**: Product differentiation, competitive pricing, maintain market share

#### 5. Decline

- Sales fall
- Competition decreases
- Profits fall
- May discontinue

**Strategy**: Reduce costs, phase out, or reinvent product

### Extension Strategies

**Methods to extend maturity stage**:

1. **Product modification**: Add features, improve quality
2. **Find new markets**: Export internationally
3. **New uses**: Promote alternative applications
4. **Rebranding**: Update image
5. **Price reduction**: Attract price-sensitive customers
6. **New packaging**: Refresh appearance
7. **Increased promotion**: Boost awareness

**Example**: Lucozade changed from illness recovery drink to sports drink
